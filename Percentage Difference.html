About Percentage Difference

The percentage difference is a measure of how much two values differ relative to their average. It's useful when comparing two values where neither is clearly the "before" or "after" value.

Formula:

Percentage Difference = (|Value1 - Value2| / ((Value1 + Value2)/2)) × 100%

When to Use:

  • Comparing two experimental results
  • Measuring variance between two similar items
  • Analyzing differences between two data points

Example Calculation:

If Value 1 is 80 and Value 2 is 100:

|80 - 100| / ((80 + 100)/2) × 100% = 20 / 90 × 100% ≈ 22.22%
